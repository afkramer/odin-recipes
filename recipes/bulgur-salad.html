Bulgur Salad

Description

A friend of mine made this delicious bulgur salad for a group of friends and we all enjoyed it so much! The key to why this recipe is so delicious is the pomegranate seeds and tons of fresh herbs. We often bring this salad to pot-luck functions and people really enjoy it! This recipe is from a German website that I can't find again, so again, sorry that I can't give credit where credit is due! But working with pomegranate can be pretty annoying.

If you've never had to get pomegranate seeds out of a pomegranate, here is the way I do it. First cut a thin slice of the top and bottom of the pomegranate off -- if you see red seeds you cut too much, but it's not a problem! Then, following the lines in the skin of the pomegranate, score the skin of the pomegranate so that you have six roughly equal sized pieces. Again, you only want to cut through the skin, not so far in that you cut through the seeds. At this point you should be able to break the fruit apart at the scored lines you've made. From there you just have to be careful and pick the seeds out. I do this over a full bowl of cold water. That way you can separate out the little bits of skin more easily. And it's always a good idea to rinse the seeds before you add them!

Ingredients

Again, since this is from a German website all the measurements are in the metric system. Feel free to make adjustments here! You can really add whatever you like -- include bell peppers, different kinds of herbs, whatever!

For the salad

  • 300 g bulgur
  • 400 ml vegetable broth
  • 150 g feta cheese
  • 2 pomegranates (processed as described above)
  • 1 bunch of spring onions
  • A few mint leaves
  • Some basil leaves
  • 1 bunch of parsley
  • A few leaves of lemon balm
  • 1 bunch of cilantro
  • Other herb ideas: chives, dill, thyme... Sky's the limit!

For the sauce

  • The juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tsp. ground coriander
  • 1 tsp. honey
  • 1 tblsp. olive oil
  • Pepper and salt to taste

Steps

  1. Let the bulgur soak in the vegetable broth until soft.
  2. Chop up all herbs finely.
  3. Break up the feta into chunks or cut into cubes as desired.
  4. Mix all salad ingredients together once the bulgur has soaked up all the broth and is soft.
  5. Mix together the dressing ingredients in a separate bowl.
  6. Pour the dressing over the salad and mix well.
  7. Serve chilled.

Serves 6-8 people.
